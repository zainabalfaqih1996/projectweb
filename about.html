IS IT A TEA OR A
TISANE?

Herbal teas, known as tisanes, have a
history as long as tea itself, but the two are
distinctly different drinks. Tisanes are
caffeine-free beverages that don’t contain
any tea.

Tisanes contain fresh or dried flowers,
leaves, fruits, seeds and/or herbs that are
steeped in boiling water. In short, they’re
not really teas at all, even though they look
like they are.

How long have tisanes been around? Since
1550 BC at least. Documents dating back to
ancient Egypt and China have mentioned
the pleasures and medicinal benefits of
herbal tea.
